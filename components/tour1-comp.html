Boating in Chilika lake




Chilika lake is the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia and the second largest coastal lagoon in the world, covering an area of over 1,100 square kilometres. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a paradise for migratory birds and wildlife lovers.

If you are looking for an unforgettable experience in nature, Chilika lake is the perfect destination for you. You will enjoy a variety of activities such as:

  • Dolphin spotting: Chilika lake is home to the endangered Irrawaddy dolphins, which can be seen in their natural habitat. You will take a boat ride to the Satapada area, where the lake meets the Bay of Bengal, and watch these graceful creatures swim and play.
  • Boating: Chilika lake offers a scenic and relaxing boating experience, where you will explore the different islands, channels, and creeks of the lagoon. You will also visit the Kalijai temple, a popular pilgrimage site located on an island in the lake.
  • Visiting sea mouth beach: Sea mouth beach is a pristine and secluded beach, where the Chilika lake flows into the sea. You will enjoy the views of the blue waters, the golden sand, and the green mangroves. You will also witness the spectacular sunset and sunrise at this beach.
  • Bird watching: Chilika lake is a haven for bird watchers, as it hosts more than 200 species of birds, both resident and migratory. You will spot flamingos, pelicans, herons, egrets, storks, cranes, and many more. The best time to visit Chilika lake for bird watching is from November to February, when thousands of birds flock to the lake.
